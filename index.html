I am a computational biology postdoc at Johns Hopkins University working between the labs of Ben Langmead and Jeff Leek. I build software for integrative analysis of many high-throughput sequencing samples to attack their systematic study on an unprecedented scale. My tools of choice are in the Hadoop ecosystem, and my flagship product is Rail-RNA, which scales to analyze thousands of RNA sequencing (RNA-seq) samples on Amazon Elastic MapReduce. Together with my advisers and other collaborators, I recently used Rail-RNA to analyze over 21,500 human RNA-seq samples from the Sequence Read Archive in the largest study of transcriptomic variation to date. We discovered thousands of unannotated exon-exon splice junctions.

I was formerly a postdoc with Jun Song at UCSF's Institute for Human Genetics, where I was the principal developer of NSeq, software for identifying positioned nucleosomes from sequencing data. I also contributed to CHANCE, software for quality control of ChIP-seq data, as well as this Cell Stem Cell study of lncRNAs of adult neural stem cells.

I used to write papers in high-energy physics theory.
